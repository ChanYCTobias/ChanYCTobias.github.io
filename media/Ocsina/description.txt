ocsina is an OCT-based clinical decision-support startup prototype that converts routine retinal scans into structured biomarker summaries, risk flags, and clinician-friendly reports for neurology workflows, starting with multiple sclerosis. The product focuses on extracting pRNFL and mGCIPL measurements from OCT data, applying quality checks, comparing results against published thresholds, tracking longitudinal change, and presenting the output in a web interface built for expert review.

For this project, I built the core pipeline across data ingestion, analysis, and product delivery. The system includes vendor-aware DICOM parsing for Heidelberg Spectralis and Zeiss Cirrus workflows, age-aware normative comparison, bilateral inter-eye difference analysis, a rules-based risk engine aligned to McDonald 2024 and related OCT/MS literature, PDF/JSON/CSV report export, and an expert-demo website that walks users through upload, analysis, risk reporting, similar cases, and longitudinal monitoring.
